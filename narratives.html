The spaces

Design as a Living Experience

This exhibition is a celebration of Italian design excellence, highlighting the dynamic intersection of form, function, and emotion across four carefully curated spaces: the study, bathroom, living room, and kitchen. Drawing from iconic pieces of the Milan Triennale, the exhibition explores how design shapes everyday life, turning ordinary environments into extraordinary experiences.

Each space tells a unique story about how we live, interact, and find beauty in our surroundings, offering a window into the profound impact of design on daily living. By stepping into each room, you enter a world where creativity meets practicality, where design becomes not just something to admire but to live in.

The spaces

The Living Room

The living room is the heart of social interaction and creativity.

Featuring a mix of bold forms like Joe Colombo’s Day Night and Marco Zanuso’s Antropus chair, the space represents a marriage of comfort and avant-garde design. Pieces such as Algol 11 television and Ettore Sottsass' Enorme embody the technological leaps of their time, balancing art and practicality.

The spaces

The Bathroom

A space often overlooked in design exhibitions, the bathroom in this collection is a reflection of Italian creativity and modern luxury.

With pieces like Shiva by Sottsass and Linda by Castiglioni, function is merged with beauty, showing that even the most personal spaces in a home deserve attention to detail.

The spaces

The Kitchen

Often considered the most important room in any household, the kitchen in this exhibition is a blend of tradition and innovation.

From the Leonardo table by Achille Castiglioni to Marco Zanuso’s Carmencita coffee maker, this space is a tribute to Italian craftsmanship and culinary culture, reflecting the simplicity and elegance that make Italian kitchens central to both home and life.

The spaces

The Study

The study offers a retreat for focus and intellectual engagement.

Featuring iconic items such as Ettore Sottsass’ M24 and Joe Colombo’s Optic, this room combines vintage aesthetics with modern utility, demonstrating how design enhances both the mind and the space it occupies.
